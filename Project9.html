Roll Cage Design For Baja SAE

Objectives

  • In this project, I designed a roll cage for the Baja SAE competition, which was part of a Structural Design subject at university. The design was created in SolidWorks, and both frontal impact and load test analyses were conducted in SolidWorks Simulation.

    • Solidworks design

  • I made a research before designing the roll cage, focusing first on meeting all legal requirements. This involved investigating material specifications, size constraints, recommended welding methods, and required contact points. By understanding these parameters early in the process, I could select appropriate materials that would ensure structural integrity while complying with competition regulations.
  • We selected a rack and pinion steering mechanism for its and responsive feedback. For suspension, we used a double wishbone configuration at the front to optimize wheel control, while choosing a semi-trailing link system for the rear suspension to balance performance with packaging constraints.

    • Frontal Impact Displacement Simulation

  • In this design, I have ultilized the DFMA and DFAM to design and manufacture all the parts in this project. I have used 2020 aluminum profile to build the main frame and I have printed most of the other part with PLA and ABS for the parts that under more stress.
  • The advantage of this design is that, it is easy to disassemble and assemble again but still keep a good precision. Most of the bolts are M5 profile, so that it is easier to maintain.

    • Load Test Displacement Simulation
