Roll Cage Design For Baja SAE

Objectives

  • In this project, I designed a roll cage for the Baja SAE competition, which was part of a Structural Design subject at university. The design was created in SolidWorks, and both frontal impact and load test analyses were conducted in SolidWorks Simulation.

    • Solidworks design

  • I made a research before designing the roll cage, focusing on meeting all legal requirements. This involved investigating material specifications, size constraints, welding methods, and required contact points. By understanding these parameters early in the process, I could select appropriate materials that would ensure structural integrity while complying with competition regulations.
  • We selected a rack and pinion steering mechanism for its and responsive feedback. For suspension, we used a double wishbone configuration at the front to optimize wheel control, while choosing a semi-trailing link system for the rear suspension to balance performance with packaging constraints.

    • Frontal Impact Displacement Simulation

  • I ran a simulation for frontal impact with a 4600N force applied to the front bracing. The roll cage's estimated weight is 30kg, with the reaction force calculated assuming the crash will deform the vehicle by 12.5mm before coming to a complete stop, providing a safety margin. Using the formula F = mvÂ²/(2d), with a velocity of 60m/s, the simulation results show a maximum displacement of only 2.42mm, indicating a safety factor of approximately 6.

    • Load Test Displacement Simulation

  • In this test, I conducted a load simulation with an 80kg person seated in the middle, along with a 5kg front load and a 25kg rear load. The results demonstrated a maximum displacement of approximately 0.8mm, which is negligibly small.
